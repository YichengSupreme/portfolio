Investigating drug synergy in acute myeloid leukaemia

During my research in Professor Yang Shi's lab at Oxford, I am currently working with Dr. Qianpeng Li, applying computational approaches to integrate public cancer datasets (such as DepMap and BeatAML) with experimental data. Present

Benchmark study for emotion recognition from multimodal neurophysiological data

As the first step toward my long-term initiative, the Memory Recorder (see Projects: Future), I set out to assess the ability of current machine learning models to predict emotions from EEG data, using this project as a bridge from biochemistry into neuroscience. While working with the DREAMER dataset, I found that the task is more challenging than some published studies suggest, which led me to reframe the project into a benchmark study. I am now comparing the predictive power of EEG, ECG, and combined EEG–ECG data under sample-independent, sample-dependent, and few-shot conditions, and I plan to validate my pipeline on an independent dataset such as SEED. This work has given me practical experience in signal preprocessing, feature extraction, and model evaluation, and lays the foundation for future efforts in real-time emotional state decoding.
