Drug Synergy in Acute Myeloid Leukaemia

Computational approaches to integrate public cancer datasets with experimental data

Project Overview

During my research in Professor Yang Shi's lab at Oxford, I work closely with the capable bioinformatician Dr. Qianpeng Li, applying computational approaches to integrate public cancer datasets with experimental data.

I analysed public gene expression, drug response, CRISPR, RNAi as well as patient-level data to devise better stratification of AML patients and drugs that should be administered to them accordingly.

This experience has been transformative in shaping how I think about science. Through long discussions with Dr. Qianpeng Li, I was exposed to a bioinformatician’s way of reasoning — approaching problems from a top-down, system-level perspective, with as few assumptions as possible. Coming from a wet-lab biochemistry background, I found this to be a uniquely different and highly valuable way of thinking, one that has broadened my approach to formulating scientific questions.

Alongside data analysis, I was also offered the opportunity to design a pipeline tool for the lab, reflecting both my growing computational skills and my prior experience with app development. This project demonstrated that I can not only analyze biological data but also build reusable computational pipelines — an ability that I believe will be valuable to many research groups. More broadly, I see the combination of my biochemical training and my developing bioinformatic perspective as highly transferable to neuroscience, where chemical and electrical viewpoints must increasingly be bridged.
