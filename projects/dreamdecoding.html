Dream Decoding

Advanced neural decoding and dream state analysis for understanding consciousness

Project Overview

My interest in dream decoding is inspired both by my own vivid dream experiences and by the mind-opening work of Adam Haar Horowitz and his device Dormio, which demonstrated that external inputs can influence dream content. In the near term, I am interested in how dream decoding might support therapeutic applications — for example, reducing nightmares, treating trauma, and enhancing memory consolidation during sleep.

More broadly, I see dreams as a language we do not yet understand — an internal narrative that remains largely inaccessible due to the “problem of other minds.” By developing tools that identify and align signals shared across dream states, we may begin to decode this language. In the long term, this could open possibilities for more precise engineering of dream content. While such advances might open up transformative applications such as dream advertising or seeing a passed away loved one, it may also warrant ethical considerations such as “dream advertisment”. I believe these ethical considerations should be confronted early, even as the scientific foundations are still being built.
