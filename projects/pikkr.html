Pikkr

A minimal decision-making app with adaptive preference learning

Project Overview

Pikkr is a lightweight decision-making app on iOS App Store I built to explore simple tools for reducing decision fatigue. The idea was inspired by a very practical challenge — not always knowing what to eat each day — which led me to design a system that could make small, repeated decisions easier. The current version allows users to create option decks and perform randomized selection, implemented in Flutter for cross-platform use. Alongside this, I have begun testing adaptive algorithms — including a multi-armed bandit framework and the potential for lightweight LSTM integration — to enable the app to learn from user preferences over time.

Though I still have plans to implement more advanced features, Pikkr has given me hands-on experience in mobile app design, probabilistic reasoning. It also sparked my interest in human–computer interaction, by showing me how even small algorithmic choices can influence user decision-making.

While Pikkr was valuable as a first coding project and has great sentimental value, I eventually recognized its limitations. For trivial, low-stakes choices, people can simply pick at random without an app or ask a friend; for meaningful decisions, they tend to seek informed suggestions grounded in evidence or expert systems rather than a simple preference-weighted algorithm. This left Pikkr in an awkward middle ground — neither playful enough to be essential for casual use, nor robust enough to guide serious decisions. Reflecting on this helped me sharpen my judgment about what constitutes a “real problem,” and guided me to focus on projects with greater scientific and practical impact.
