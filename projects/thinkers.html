Thinkers

Computational modeling to explore philosophical questions about biological and social systems

Project Overview

Thinkers is a collection of mini-projects driven by the shared similarity I see between "cell and organism" and "individual and society." Through computational modeling, I explore some of my biggest philosophical questions about how complex systems emerge from simpler components.

This project bridges my interests in biochemistry, neuroscience, and social dynamics, using mathematical frameworks to investigate patterns that appear across different scales of organization. Each mini-project tackles a specific question about emergence, adaptation, and the nature of collective behavior.

Ambition and Clan

Is the meaning of a person predetermined, or is it self-created in the process of life?

Why do some individuals devote their lives to grand, civilization-scale projects — while others find meaning in the intimacy of family, community, or craft? Neither path is "better"; both are vital. But is there an optimal balance between these archetypes — dreamers and stabilizers — that allows a society to thrive?

This project explores that question through a generative, agent-based model that simulates the co-evolution of ambition, influence, and ideology across a population of interacting "clans." It is a sandbox for testing whether certain distributions of ambition are self-stabilizing — and what macro-patterns (empires, equilibria, or chaos) arise when microscopic social rules unfold over time.

The model includes scripts showing how clans can form naturally based on ambition similarity and how innovation-driven assimilation can drive clan expansion and competition. Another component is a clan dynamics model that incorporates power-weighted assimilation and ambition-driven influence. Each clan member has an ideology color in a RGB space, ambition level, and influence level that determine how they interact with neighbors and spread their ideology.

I systematically test different conditions against a baseline script to model the effects of various parameters on clan formation, stability, and macro-pattern emergence. This allows me to explore how different social rules and environmental factors influence the balance between dreamers and stabilizers in simulated societies.

Each script provides a 2D visualization that shows the spatial distribution of clan members, their ideology colors, and how ambition and influence levels evolve over time. These visualizations help reveal the emergent patterns and dynamics that arise from the microscopic social rules.
